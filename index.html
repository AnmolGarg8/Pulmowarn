Healthy Lung Function

A perfect
biological
pump.

Coordinated cilia sweep mucus upward continuously. Air flows freely into the alveoli. Carbon dioxide exits cleanly. The body maintains perfect chemical balance.

Infection Strikes

Mucus fills the
narrowing

Infection paralyses cilia and triggers 10× mucus overproduction. The airway narrows from the inside. The patient feels it — but has no way to know how dangerous it has become.

CO₂ Narcosis

Carbon dioxide
silences
the brain.

CO₂ crosses the blood-brain barrier freely. First headache at 55 mmHg. Drowsiness at 65. Unconsciousness above 80 mmHg. This is CO₂ narcosis — the patient sedated by their own chemistry.

35–45 mmHg Normal
45–55 mmHg Early retention
55–65 mmHg Headache, confusion
65–75 mmHg Drowsiness
75+ mmHg Loss of consciousness

Pulse Oximeter

Measures oxygen only. Completely blind to CO₂. A patient can show 98% SpO₂ while PaCO₂ climbs to narcotic levels. Millions trust it. It measures the wrong thing.

Hospital Monitors

Clinical CO₂ monitors like the SenTec are accurate. But at $10,000+ and requiring trained staff to operate, they are not designed for a patient managing lung disease at home at 2 AM.

NOT HOME-DEPLOYABLE

Sputum Monitoring

Nothing on the home market detects sputum accumulation. No device alerts a patient that their airway is narrowing before CO₂ rises. This is the earliest and most actionable point in the deterioration chain.
