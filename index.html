FREQUENTLY ASKED

Questions we always get

Is PulmoCare FDA approved?

PulmoCare is currently in the pre-clinical prototype phase. The identified regulatory pathway is FDA 510(k) Class II clearance, with predicate comparison available for both acoustic monitoring and CO₂ sensing modalities. Submission is planned for Phase 3 (2026–2027) following clinical validation.

Has it been tested on real patients?

The current stage is bench-level prototype validation. The acoustic classifier has been tested against the ICBHI 2017 Respiratory Sound Database. A pilot clinical study with n=30–40 COPD patients is planned for Phase 2 (Q3 2025–Q1 2026) in partnership with a hospital respiratory medicine department.

Why not just use a pulse oximeter?

Pulse oximeters measure oxygen saturation only. They are completely blind to CO₂. A patient on supplemental oxygen can maintain 97–98% SpO₂ while blood CO₂ climbs toward narcotic levels. Additionally, no pulse oximeter can detect sputum accumulation. PulmoCare monitors the two variables that pulse oximetry misses entirely.

How is patient data kept private?

All signal processing happens locally on the ARM Cortex-M4 MCU. Raw audio and CO₂ readings are never transmitted. Only alert events and anonymised trend summaries are sent via BLE to the companion app. Cloud storage (Phase 2+) uses AES-256 at rest and TLS 1.3 in transit on a HIPAA-eligible AWS architecture.

When can I get one?

PulmoCare is not yet commercially available. We are seeking clinical partners, investors, and institutional support to accelerate Phase 2 validation. If you are a respiratory clinician, hospital administrator, or investor, please reach out directly at anmol.garg@vips.edu . Target commercial availability is 2027 subject to regulatory clearance.
